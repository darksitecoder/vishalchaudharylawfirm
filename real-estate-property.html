Courts have consistently held that clarity of title and lawful transfer are fundamental to maintaining certainty and confidence in property transactions.

Ownership, Title, and Possession

Legal ownership of immovable property flows from valid title supported by lawful transfer and proper registration. Possession alone does not confer ownership unless supported by statutory requirements.

In Suraj Lamp & Industries Pvt. Ltd. v. State of Haryana (2012), the Supreme Court categorically held that GPA sales do not convey ownership, reafOfficeing the necessity of registered conveyance deeds.

Our Office conducts exhaustive title verification, tracing ownership history to ensure clients acquire legally marketable property.

Transfer of Property and Conveyancing

Transfer of immovable property may occur through sale, gift, exchange, mortgage, or lease, each governed by specific provisions of the Transfer of Property Act, 1882.

The Supreme Court in Nahalchand Laloochand Pvt. Ltd. v. Panchali Co-operative Housing Society Ltd. (2010) clarified that common areas cannot be sold independently by developers.

Vishal Chaudhary’s Law Office provides legally robust drafting and vetting of sale deeds, conveyance deeds, gift deeds, and mortgage instruments.

Registration, Stamp Duty, and Legal Documentation

Registration of property documents under the Registration Act, 1908 is mandatory to confer legal validity and enforceability. Stamp duty compliance is equally critical to avoid penalties and litigation.

In Thomson Press (India) Ltd. v. Nanak Builders (2013), the Supreme Court emphasized that unregistered documents cannot affect immovable property rights.

Our Office ensures complete statutory compliance to eliminate future legal vulnerabilities.

RERA Compliance and Builder-Buyer Disputes

The Real Estate (Regulation and Development) Act, 2016 was enacted to promote transparency, accountability, and consumer protection in the real estate sector.

In Pioneer Urban Land & Infrastructure Ltd. v. Union of India (2019), the Supreme Court upheld RERA’s constitutional validity and strengthened homebuyer rights.

Vishal Chaudhary’s Law Office represents clients before RERA Authorities, Appellate Tribunals, and High Courts in matters involving delayed possession, defective construction, and refund claims.

Leases, Tenancy, and Eviction Matters

Lease agreements govern landlord-tenant relationships and are regulated under the Transfer of Property Act and state-specific rent control laws.

Courts have consistently emphasized that lease terms must be interpreted strictly in accordance with contractual intent and statutory provisions.
